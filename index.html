PhD Research Chemist

About

I am currently working in David Wei's group at the University of Florida designing new nanomaterials for photocatalytic applications. I hold a PhD from UF where I developed plasmonic materials for solar photocatalysis. I have a passion for renewable energy, technology and have a keen eye for detail.

Research

Materials

Designed photocatalysts with specific physical and electronic properties through interfacing of metals (e.g. Au, Ag, Cu) and semiconductors (e.g. TiO2, ZnO, SrTiO3, WO3, ZrO2)

Instrumentation

Designed, built, and currently maintain a customized batch reactor based on gas chromatography which enables wavelength-dependent characterization of photocatalysts during reaction which runs on custom LabVIEW software

Photocatalysis

Developed strategies to elucidate enhancement mechanisms in plasmonic photocatalysts involving Fourier-transform infrared spectroscopy, gas chromatography, photoelectrochemistry, Raman spectroscopy

Outreach

Coordinated interactive lab demonstrations to convey chemistry concepts to visiting high school students and the Gainesville community through: Molecular Mania - Chemistry Day at Oaks Mall, STEM Immersion Experience at UF, and Chemathon programs
